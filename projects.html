The story

Why I built FluxoOps

FluxoOps started from a real conversation about the future of IT: as AI accelerates, traditional ticketing is being replaced by smarter, more automated workflows. I wanted to translate this future into something tangible — a tool that combines the discipline of ITIL with the agility of AIOps.

The goal was never to clone ServiceNow or Jira. It was to design from scratch a focused, opinionated, and elegant ITSM platform that solves the real pain points I see daily in IT operations: slow triage, scattered service catalogs, lost devices in international shipping, and end users who don't know where to start.

Key features

What's inside

✦

AIOps Categorization

Smart engine that analyzes ticket content and proposes type, category, sub-category, priority and team — with full manual override.

⚙

ITIL-aligned types

Full separation of Incidents, Service Requests and Changes, with RFC-specific fields like change window, risk and rollback plan.

📦

Asset Logistics

Track international device shipping with a visual pipeline (preparing → delivered → registered in CMDB) and a global destinations mini-map.

📚

Knowledge Base

Curated articles with markdown rendering, plus live AI-driven suggestions while users type their tickets — to deflect avoidable requests.

👥

Role-based UX

Operators see the full ops dashboard and tools. End users see a simple self-service portal — with their own tickets and devices only.

🌍

Bilingual native

Full English + Portuguese support across every screen, message, category and seed data — designed for multinational companies.

Tech stack

Built with

Modern frontend stack with no backend dependency — fully client-side so the demo can be experienced without any setup.
